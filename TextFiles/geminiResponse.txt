## Course Title: Frontiers in Cancer Cell Research: From Mechanisms to Emerging Therapies

## Prerequisites

*   **Basic Biology:** A foundational understanding of cell biology, including cell structure, function, and processes like cell division, DNA replication, and protein synthesis.
*   **Genetics:** Familiarity with basic genetics concepts, including DNA, RNA, genes, chromosomes, and mutations.
*   **Molecular Biology:** Understanding of molecular biology principles, including gene expression, regulation, and signaling pathways.
*   **Basic Chemistry:** A grasp of basic chemical principles relevant to biology, such as the structure of organic molecules and chemical reactions.
*   **Introductory Immunology (Optional but Recommended):** A basic understanding of the immune system and its role in recognizing and eliminating abnormal cells.
*   **Ability to Read and Interpret Scientific Literature:** Familiarity with scientific terminology and the structure of research papers.

## Course Content

**Module 1: Cancer Cell Hallmarks Revisited & Emerging Perspectives**

*   **1.1 The Classical Hallmarks of Cancer:** Review of the traditional hallmarks of cancer (sustaining proliferative signaling, evading growth suppressors, resisting cell death, enabling replicative immortality, inducing angiogenesis, activating invasion and metastasis) and their underlying molecular mechanisms.
    *   Focus on recent discoveries that refine or challenge these original hallmarks.
*   **1.2 Emerging Hallmarks:** Exploration of newly recognized hallmarks of cancer, such as:
    *   **Deregulation of Cellular Energetics:** Warburg effect and metabolic reprogramming in cancer cells.
        *   Newer understanding of mitochondrial function and dysfunction in cancer.
    *   **Genome Instability and Mutation:** The role of genomic instability in cancer development and progression.
        *   Focus on DNA repair mechanisms and how they are often compromised in cancer cells.
    *   **Tumor-Promoting Inflammation:** The interplay between cancer cells and the immune system, with a focus on chronic inflammation.
        *   The role of cytokines and chemokines in tumor microenvironment.
    *   **Unlocking Phenotypic Plasticity:** Cancer stem cells, epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT), and cellular plasticity as drivers of tumor progression and resistance.
*   **1.3 The Tumor Microenvironment (TME):** Detailed analysis of the TME, including:
    *   Cellular components (fibroblasts, immune cells, endothelial cells) and their interactions with cancer cells.
    *   Extracellular matrix (ECM) remodeling and its impact on cancer progression.
    *   The role of the TME in drug resistance.

**Module 2: Advanced Cancer Genomics and Epigenomics**

*   **2.1 Cancer Genomics:** Exploring the landscape of cancer genomes through high-throughput sequencing technologies:
    *   Whole-genome sequencing (WGS), whole-exome sequencing (WES), and RNA sequencing (RNA-Seq).
    *   Identification of driver mutations and passenger mutations.
    *   Cancer genome atlases (e.g., TCGA, ICGC) and their impact on cancer research.
*   **2.2 Epigenetics in Cancer:** Understanding the role of epigenetic modifications in cancer development and progression:
    *   DNA methylation, histone modifications, and non-coding RNAs.
    *   Epigenetic therapies and their potential for cancer treatment.
*   **2.3 Single-Cell Omics:** Introduction to single-cell genomics and transcriptomics:
    *   Analyzing cellular heterogeneity within tumors.
    *   Identifying rare cancer cell populations.
    *   Understanding the dynamics of cancer cell evolution.
*   **2.4 Liquid Biopsies:** Exploring the use of liquid biopsies for cancer diagnosis and monitoring:
    *   Circulating tumor cells (CTCs), circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA), and exosomes.
    *   Applications of liquid biopsies in personalized medicine.

**Module 3: Cancer Metabolism and Signaling Pathways**

*   **3.1 Metabolic Reprogramming in Cancer:** In-depth analysis of metabolic alterations in cancer cells:
    *   The Warburg effect and its implications for cancer metabolism.
    *   Glutamine metabolism, lipid metabolism, and amino acid metabolism in cancer cells.
    *   Targeting cancer metabolism for therapeutic intervention.
*   **3.2 Deregulated Signaling Pathways:** Detailed exploration of key signaling pathways involved in cancer:
    *   PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathway, Ras/MAPK pathway, Wnt/Î²-catenin pathway, and Notch pathway.
    *   Targeting signaling pathways for cancer therapy.
*   **3.3 Crosstalk Between Metabolism and Signaling:** Understanding the interplay between metabolic pathways and signaling pathways in cancer cells:
    *   Metabolic regulation of signaling pathways.
    *   Signaling regulation of metabolic pathways.
    *   Therapeutic strategies targeting both metabolism and signaling.

**Module 4: The Cancer-Immune Interaction and Immunotherapy**

*   **4.1 The Immune System and Cancer:** Review of the immune system's role in recognizing and eliminating cancer cells:
    *   Innate immunity, adaptive immunity, and immune surveillance.
    *   Immune evasion mechanisms of cancer cells.
*   **4.2 Cancer Immunotherapy:** Exploring different approaches to harness the immune system to fight cancer:
    *   Immune checkpoint inhibitors (e.g., anti-PD-1, anti-CTLA-4).
    *   CAR-T cell therapy.
    *   Cancer vaccines.
    *   Oncolytic viruses.
*   **4.3 Resistance to Immunotherapy:** Understanding the mechanisms of resistance to immunotherapy:
    *   Tumor microenvironment factors.
    *   Immune cell dysfunction.
    *   Strategies to overcome resistance to immunotherapy.
*   **4.4 Combinatorial Immunotherapy:** Exploring the potential of combining different immunotherapy approaches to improve outcomes.

**Module 5: Novel Therapeutic Strategies and Drug Development**

*   **5.1 Targeted Therapies:** Review of targeted therapies that specifically target cancer cells:
    *   Small molecule inhibitors, monoclonal antibodies, and antibody-drug conjugates.
    *   Personalized medicine and biomarker-driven drug development.
*   **5.2 Novel Drug Targets:** Exploration of emerging drug targets in cancer cells:
    *   Non-coding RNAs, epigenetic modifiers, and metabolic enzymes.
*   **5.3 Drug Resistance Mechanisms:** Understanding the mechanisms of drug resistance:
    *   Genetic mutations, epigenetic modifications, and altered signaling pathways.
    *   Strategies to overcome drug resistance.
*   **5.4 Emerging Therapeutic Modalities:** Introduction to novel therapeutic modalities:
    *   CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing, RNA interference (RNAi), and nanotechnology-based drug delivery.
*   **5.5 Clinical Trials:** Overview of the clinical trial process and the evaluation of new cancer therapies.
